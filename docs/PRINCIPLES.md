# Servolia Operating Principles (Zero to One)

These principles govern every product, marketing, pricing, and expansion decision at Servolia.
Derived from Peter Thiel's *Zero to One*. When a decision conflicts with a principle, the principle wins
unless we consciously rewrite it here.

---

## P1 — Monopoly, not competition

We do not try to be a *better* AI receptionist agency. We build something with **no close substitute**
for one specific audience.

- Never pitch Servolia by comparing to competitors ("cheaper than X", "better than a chatbot").
  Pitch the unique problem only we solve.
- If a prospect can name a close substitute for what we offer them, our offer is wrong — narrow it
  until they can't.
- **The monopoly test (ask quarterly):** could we raise prices 30% without losing our current clients?
  If no, we are in competition, not monopoly. Fix differentiation before chasing growth.

## P2 — Own a tiny market completely before touching a bigger one

Beachhead: **Franco-Maghrebi women-owned laser & premium aesthetic centres in Maghrebi-dense areas
of 2–3 French cities (Paris/93, Lyon, Marseille), already running Meta ads.** ~100–300 businesses.

- Success metric is **share of the niche**, not revenue: 10 of ~150 reachable centres beats 10 clients
  scattered across 10 verticals.
- We do NOT take clients outside the current rung of the ladder, even if they pay. Every off-ladder
  client dilutes the playbook, the case-study bank, and the referral network.
- Expansion ladder (move only after clear dominance of the current rung):
  1. Laser / premium aesthetic centres (current)
  2. Hair transplant + cosmetic dentistry, incl. the France→Turkey/Morocco medical-travel funnel
  3. Premium Maghrebi bridal (planners, venues, makeup, photo)
  4. Generic French aesthetic centres (arrive with 30 case studies, not a pitch)
  - Explicitly excluded: legal, recruitment, real estate, low-budget solo beauty pros.

## P3 — 10x on one dimension, not 20% on many

Our 10x is not "faster replies." It is the bundle no one else can assemble:

1. **Bilingual AI intake — native French + Darija/Arabic.** Not "better" than French SaaS; *unavailable*
   anywhere else. Every flow we ship must handle code-switched FR/Darija messages naturally.
2. **Closed loop with paid ads.** We run the ads AND the intake, so we show the owner the number no
   one has ever shown her: which euro of ad spend became which booked consultation.
3. Anything that is only 20% better than a GHL template does not go on the roadmap.

## P4 — Build the moat structurally, from day one

Map every roadmap item to at least one of Thiel's four monopoly features:

- **Proprietary tech/knowledge:** the conversation corpus. Every client conversation feeds a
  vertical-specific qualification playbook (objection patterns, price anchors, no-show triggers for
  this exact clientele). This data is the asset — instrument, store, and mine it from client #1.
- **Network effects:** the niche is socially dense (owners follow and know each other). Design for
  referral: case studies with real numbers, client WhatsApp community, "recovered revenue" screenshots
  owners want to share.
- **Economies of scale:** one repeatable implementation per rung. No bespoke builds. Setup time per
  client must fall with every install; if client #5 takes as long as client #1, we built services,
  not software.
- **Brand:** "Servolia" = the system that never loses an enquiry, in the client's own language.
  FR line: *« Servolia répond à chaque demande Instagram/WhatsApp en 30 secondes — en français comme
  en darija — et la transforme en consultation réservée. »* Brand claims must sit on measured results,
  never ahead of them.

## P5 — Definite optimism: plan, don't iterate blindly

- We have a definite plan (the ladder above). We don't pivot on one bad month or one loud prospect.
- Changes to the ladder or the beachhead require a written case in this file — not a vibe.

## P6 — Distribution is half the product

- Price point (€1.5–3k setup, €299–699/mo) is Thiel's distribution dead zone; the escape is
  **founder-led sales with proof**: mystery-shop the prospect, record the missed lead, Loom the owner.
- First 3 clients in any new rung: discounted setup traded for a numbered case study. The case study
  *is* the product of month one.
- Sales effort compounds only inside the niche (referrals, word of mouth). Off-niche sales effort is
  wasted even when it closes.

## P7 — Escape competition in language, too

- We never define ourselves as "disrupting" receptionists, Planity, or agencies. We define the
  category: **lead recovery for aesthetic centres, in the client's language**.
- Beware the fake-monopoly trap: don't intersect adjectives to feel unique ("the only AI + Darija +
  laser + Lyon tool"). The test is the customer's view: does *she* have a real alternative for the
  problem we solve? If yes, we're competing.

## P8 — Compliance as a moat, not a checkbox

- AI stays on scheduling, pricing ranges, and logistics — never medical/skin-suitability advice.
- GDPR consent in the first message of every flow (CNIL chatbot guidance).
- Doing this properly is a barrier to the fly-by-night GHL resellers; treat it as differentiation.
