# Servolia Operating Principles (Zero to One)

These principles govern every product, marketing, pricing, and expansion decision at Servolia.
Derived from Peter Thiel's *Zero to One*. When a decision conflicts with a principle, the principle wins
unless we consciously rewrite it here.

---

## P1 — Monopoly, not competition

We do not try to be a *better* AI receptionist agency. We build something with **no close substitute**
for one specific audience.

- Never pitch Servolia by comparing to competitors ("cheaper than X", "better than a chatbot").
  Pitch the unique problem only we solve.
- If a prospect can name a close substitute for what we offer them, our offer is wrong — narrow it
  until they can't.
- **The monopoly test (ask quarterly):** could we raise prices 30% without losing our current clients?
  If no, we are in competition, not monopoly. Fix differentiation before chasing growth.

## P2 — Own a tiny market completely before touching a bigger one

Beachhead: **independent dental & implant clinics in 3–5 French cities (Lyon, Bordeaux, Toulouse,
Nice, Strasbourg), with weak online conversion** — no online booking, no chat, phone-only intake,
or paying for ads without a system. ~200–500 reachable practices.

- Success metric is **share of the niche**, not revenue: 10 of ~200 reachable clinics beats 10 clients
  scattered across 10 verticals.
- We do NOT take clients outside the current rung of the ladder, even if they pay. Every off-ladder
  client dilutes the playbook, the case-study bank, and the referral network.
- Expansion ladder (move only after clear dominance of the current rung). Each rung names the
  dominant booking platform we must speak natively — this is deliberate: the platform *is* the
  distribution channel for finding and integrating with that rung's clients.
  1. **Dental / implant clinics, France (current).** Platform: Doctolib.
  2. **Aesthetic clinics & med spas, France.** Platform: **Planity** (40,000+ businesses on it,
     zero AI-qualification competitors building for it — this is the sharpest open door on the
     entire ladder). Pricing may pilot pay-per-booked-consultation here (non-physician-run
     businesses only — see P6 note).
  3. **Dental + aesthetic, Belgium & Switzerland** (same language, same playbook). Platform:
     DentAdmin (syncs with Doctena) for Belgian dental.
  4. Other high-value service verticals (arrive with 30 case studies, not a pitch)
  - Explicitly excluded: legal, recruitment, real estate, low-budget solo practitioners.
  - **Opportunistic side-lane, not a rung:** Swiss premium hair-transplant clinics (<100 total,
    FMH/MedReg registries). Highest ticket size on the ladder (€9–14k), but too small a market and
    a different regulator (Swiss nFADP) to be a *first* market. Activate only once rung 1 or 2 has
    produced real case studies — hand-pick outreach, don't systematize it like a rung.

## P3 — 10x on one dimension, not 20% on many

**The secret (validated independently, 2026-07-15 — see memory: micro-monopoly-research-2026-07):**
*Traffic is a commoditized liability; the real bottleneck is the response-time gap and the total
absence of qualification before booking.* Every competing agency (branding studios, native-ad
shops, compliance-focused comms agencies) is fighting over cheaper clicks and prettier top-of-funnel
work. None of them catch the patient who called at 1pm while the front desk was at lunch. A clinic
does not need more traffic — it needs the enquiries it's already paying for to never go unanswered
and never get booked unqualified. Use this framing verbatim in outbound copy; it is sharper than
"AI receptionist."

Our 10x is not "faster replies." It is the bundle no one else can assemble:

1. **The complete lead-to-booking system, installed in 7 days at a fixed written price.** Agencies
   take 6–12 weeks and quote hourly; SaaS tools give the clinic homework. We deliver the outcome.
2. **Closed loop with paid ads.** We run the tracking AND the intake, so we show the owner the number
   no one has ever shown them: which euro of ad spend became which booked consultation.
3. **Bilingual FR/EN AI intake** trained on the clinic's own services and prices — every after-hours
   enquiry answered and captured, never a generic bot.
4. **Qualification before booking, not after.** Doctolib and Planity are passive calendars — anyone
   can reserve a slot with zero medical/financial qualification, which is why no-show rates run high.
   We put the AI *in front of* the calendar as a gatekeeper: it qualifies, then books. This is the
   single feature the incumbent platforms structurally cannot offer themselves (it would slow down
   the exact frictionless-booking metric they're optimized for).
5. Anything that is only 20% better than a GHL template does not go on the roadmap.

**Named competitor weaknesses (French market, use as outbound ammunition):**
- *Bosphore Sense* — branding/comms agency, sells creativity, no performance guarantee. A beautiful
  brand doesn't stop a missed call.
- *Medgency* — native-ad specialist, drives traffic brilliantly, has no answer for what happens when
  that traffic hits a closed front desk at 9pm.
- *Dental Média* — compliance/ethics-focused dental comms. Makes a clinic look professional; does
  nothing about the patient who hangs up on hold.
- None of the three offer a closed-loop, qualification-first booking system. That gap is the whole
  business.

## P4 — Build the moat structurally, from day one

Map every roadmap item to at least one of Thiel's four monopoly features:

- **Proprietary tech/knowledge:** the conversation corpus. Every client conversation feeds a
  vertical-specific qualification playbook (objection patterns, price anchors, no-show triggers for
  this exact clientele). This data is the asset — instrument, store, and mine it from client #1.
- **Network effects:** the niche is socially dense (owners follow and know each other). Design for
  referral: case studies with real numbers, client WhatsApp community, "recovered revenue" screenshots
  owners want to share.
- **Economies of scale:** one repeatable implementation per rung. No bespoke builds. Setup time per
  client must fall with every install; if client #5 takes as long as client #1, we built services,
  not software.
- **Brand:** "Servolia" = the system that never loses an enquiry.
  FR line: *« Servolia répond à chaque demande en 30 secondes, 24h/24 — et la transforme en
  rendez-vous réservé. »* Brand claims must sit on measured results, never ahead of them.

## P5 — Definite optimism: plan, don't iterate blindly

- We have a definite plan (the ladder above). We don't pivot on one bad month or one loud prospect.
- Changes to the ladder or the beachhead require a written case in this file — not a vibe.

## P6 — Distribution is half the product

- Our price point (€290–990 setup, €49–199/mo) sits below Thiel's distribution dead zone by design —
  low enough for a productized close, raised later from a monopoly position (P1 test). The sales
  motion is **founder-led with proof**: mystery-shop the prospect, record the missed lead, Loom the owner.
- First 3 clients in any new rung: discounted setup traded for a numbered case study. The case study
  *is* the product of month one.
- Sales effort compounds only inside the niche (referrals, word of mouth). Off-niche sales effort is
  wasted even when it closes.
- **Pay-per-booked-consultation pricing (piloted on top of the flat setup fee) is a live option for
  rung 2 (aesthetic/med-spa) only, and only for businesses not run by a regulated medical
  professional.** Do not offer it to dentists/physicians without a French avocat confirming it
  doesn't read as "compérage" (referral-fee arrangements tied to patient volume are restricted for
  regulated health professions under Ordre des Chirurgiens-Dentistes / Ordre des Médecins rules).
  Flat setup + flat monthly stays the default everywhere until that's confirmed.

## P7 — Escape competition in language, too

- We never define ourselves as "disrupting" receptionists, Doctolib, or agencies. We define the
  category: **lead recovery for dental clinics**.
- Beware the fake-monopoly trap: don't intersect adjectives to feel unique ("the only AI +
  implants + Lyon tool"). The test is the customer's view: does the owner have a real alternative
  for the problem we solve? If yes, we're competing.

## P8 — Compliance as a moat, not a checkbox

- AI stays on scheduling, pricing ranges, and logistics — never medical/skin-suitability advice.
- GDPR consent in the first message of every flow (CNIL chatbot guidance).
- Doing this properly is a barrier to the fly-by-night GHL resellers; treat it as differentiation.
